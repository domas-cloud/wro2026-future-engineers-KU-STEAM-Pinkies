## Core Idea
One of the proposed steering concepts is a mechanism based on **three interconnected gears**. This approach aims to provide precise control of the front wheel steering angle and ensure smooth maneuvering on the track.

The main idea is that a central gear receives the steering input and transfers its rotation evenly to two outer gears. This keeps both sides synchronized and allows the steering angle to change proportionally to the control signal.

## Conceptual Operation Principle
The steering input would rotate the central gear, which then simultaneously transfers movement to the two outer gears. Through  linkages, this rotation would adjust the angle of the front wheel assembly.

This would allow the robot to:
- perform precise turns,
- smoothly correct its direction,
- maintain stability even with small steering adjustments.
